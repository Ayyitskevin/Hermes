# THE HERMES LEARNING PATH
### A ~90-Day Practice Curriculum — *Five-Tool Confluence AIO v3.5-SHORT*
**Desk Manual · Companion to *The Hermes Playbook* and the *Chart Reading Guide***

> **The whole curriculum in one line:** **1 strategy · 3 timeframes · 1 side · 100 journaled reps.** You are not here to collect setups. You are here to run *one* process until reading it is muscle memory — and to build a sample large enough to tell you the truth about yourself.

> **Read this first.** This is a schedule for *practice*, not a promise. Finishing Day 90 does not make you competent, and it does not make you profitable — nothing does. Reps done honestly *tilt the odds* that your execution becomes reliable over a large sample. The calendar is a floor for how much deliberate practice the work takes, never a guarantee that the time spent bought you skill or an edge. If you're not ready to advance, you're not ready — the gate outranks the date, every time.

This document assumes you have already read, and will keep open beside you:
- **The Hermes Playbook** — *how to play the thesis* (doctrine, triggers, sizing, validation).
- **The Chart Reading Guide** — *how to read the chart* (every panel, color, arrow, and number).

This curriculum is the third leg: *how to build the reps*. It does not re-teach the triggers or the HUD — it tells you the order to learn them in, and the gates that stop you from running ahead of your evidence.

---

## The Shape of the 90 Days

Four phases. Each one builds a **single skill**, has a **daily/weekly routine**, and ends at a **gate you cannot buy your way past with time**. You advance when the gate opens, not when the calendar says so.

| Phase | Window | The one skill it builds | Money at risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 · Setup** | Days 1–3 | Knowing where everything is | None |
| **1 · Read** | Weeks 1–4 | Recognizing a real setup on sight | None (replay + paper) |
| **2 · Size** | Weeks 5–8 | Executing the plan under real risk | Tiny live longs |
| **3 · Judge** | Weeks 9–12+ | Reading your own sample honestly | Tiny live longs, unchanged |

**Longs first, always.** Every phase below is a **long-side** phase. The long book is the tested workhorse; the short book is an **unvalidated experiment that ran net-negative in the reference backtest** (Playbook §7). Shorts do not appear in this curriculum until Phase 3, and even then only as a *decision to consider a validation campaign* — never as a trade you take to feel productive.

**Depth over breadth, restated.** The four long triggers *are* your "five strategies." You will be tempted, around Week 3, to go find a second setup, a second symbol, a second timeframe combination. Don't. The person who runs one process 100 times learns more than the person who runs five processes 20 times each. Breadth is how you avoid the discomfort of getting good at one thing.

## Phase 0 — Setup *(Days 1–3)*

**Goal.** Remove every excuse. When practice starts on Day 4, nothing is missing, nothing is unfamiliar, and you never have to stop mid-rep to go find something.

**The one skill it builds:** *knowing where everything is* — so that during a rep your attention is on the tape, not the tooling.

### Do this, in order
- [ ] Load the **Five-Tool Confluence AIO v3.5-SHORT** strategy on your platform.
- [ ] Save the **🟢 PURE BULL** chart config exactly as specified in Playbook §8: `Allow shorts` **OFF**, all four long triggers **ON**, `min_score` **55**, defaults. This is your only chart for Phases 1 and 2. Do not save the Bear or Hybrid configs yet — you don't need them, and having them visible invites drift.

  > ⚠️ **Shorts ship ON.** In this build `Allow shorts` defaults to **ON** (the input tooltip even says so). A longs-first student who loads raw defaults will have the short book live from bar one. Turning it **OFF** in the Pure Bull config above is not optional housekeeping — it *is* Phases 1–2.
- [ ] Build the **three-timeframe stack** (Playbook §4): Weekly (the Tide), Daily (the Trade), 1-Hour (the Time). Save the layout so it opens the same way every session.
- [ ] Read **The Hermes Playbook** end to end. You are not memorizing it; you are learning what's in it so you can find a rule when you need it.
- [ ] Read the **Chart Reading Guide** with the Pure Bull chart open beside you. Point at each HUD cell as you read its section. Do the "first 5 seconds" drill (Guide §1) ten times until Regime → Score → Halt is automatic.
- [ ] Set up your **trade journal** with the fields from Playbook §12. A spreadsheet is fine. It just has to exist before your first rep.
- [ ] Write your **pre-committed rules** somewhere you'll see them every session — the Playbook §13 "Rules of Engagement" is the template. Deciding these now, cold, is the point; deciding them mid-trade is how you lose.

### Do-not-advance-until gate
- [ ] You can name every cell in the Main HUD without the guide.
- [ ] You can state the go/no-go in one breath: **Regime Bull, Score ≥ 55, Halt = Ready — or no trade.**
- [ ] Your journal exists and your rules are written down.

> If you can't do the 5-second read from memory yet, stay in Phase 0. This is the cheapest phase to be slow in.

## Phase 1 — Read *(Weeks 1–4)*

**Goal.** Learn to recognize a **real long setup** on sight — and, just as important, to recognize the far more common "candidate that is not a setup" (Playbook §3) and pass on it. Zero money at risk. This is where you make your cheap mistakes.

**The one skill it builds:** *pattern recognition of the four long triggers* — flip, hidden divergence, regular divergence, AVWAP reclaim (Playbook §5) — inside a genuine Bull regime, read top-down across the timeframe stack.

**Environment:** **REPLAY mode and paper only.** No live orders. Replay lets you get a month of reps in an afternoon and, crucially, lets you *be wrong for free*.

### Daily routine (aim for a focused session, most days)
1. **Top-down first, every time** (Playbook §4). Weekly for the Tide, Daily for the Trade, 1H for the Time. If the Weekly isn't Bull, you already know most Daily longs are fighting the tide — note it and move on.
2. **Bar-replay** a Daily chart forward. Each time a long trigger fires, **pause before the outcome.**
3. **Journal the thesis *before* you reveal the next bars** (Playbook §12). Freeze Regime + RS + Score, name which of the four triggers fired, write the entry, the ATR×2 stop, and your planned R. Do this *first*, or the journal is just you remembering yourself as smarter than you were.
4. **Step the replay forward** and record the exit and realized R.
5. **Separate the two verdicts.** Log the P&L, then — on its own line — log the *thesis verdict*: yes / partial / no. "Was I right about the setup" is a different question from "did the trade pay," and conflating them is how you learn the wrong lesson.
6. **One-line lesson.** The only part that compounds.

### Weekly routine
- Re-read the trades you journaled. Tag each trigger type. Which are you reading well? Which fool you?
- Confirm you are still on **Pure Bull only**. If you caught yourself peeking at shorts or the Hybrid chart, write down why — that urge is data about your discipline.

### Do-not-advance-until gate
- [ ] **~30–40 journaled replay/paper reps**, every one with a *pre-outcome* thesis.
- [ ] You can tell a **≥ 55 confluence setup** from a sub-55 candidate *without looking at the score first* — then check the score to confirm you were right.
- [ ] You reliably **stand down** when the regime is Neutral/Bear, chop is High, RS is a laggard, or the halt is active (Playbook §5). Passing good-looking-but-wrong trades is the skill here, not taking trades.
- [ ] Your journal shows the **thesis verdict recorded separately from P&L** on every rep.

> **Not a gate:** a green equity curve in replay. Replay P&L is not evidence of anything — it's a practice field. The gate is *quality and honesty of reps*, not their outcome.

## Phase 2 — Size *(Weeks 5–8)*

**Goal.** Move the process into **real risk**, at a size small enough that the money can't hijack your judgment — and prove you can execute the *entire* plan (entry, stop, scale, trail, exit) when it's live. Begin building the sample that Phase 3 will judge.

**The one skill it builds:** *sizing and execution discipline* — `size% = risk% ÷ stop-distance%`, the stop sets the size, and you think in **R, not money**, start to finish (Playbook §10).

**Environment:** **tiny live longs** on the Pure Bull chart. "Tiny" means a fraction of your normal per-trade risk — small enough that a string of losses is boring, not frightening. You are buying the experience of live execution, and the price of that tuition should be trivial. Still **longs only. Still Pure Bull only.**

### Per-trade discipline (non-negotiable, from the Playbook)
- **Risk is a percent of equity, never a dollar figure.** If you catch yourself thinking in dollars, stop and re-frame in % and R (Playbook §3, §10).
- **The stop sets the size.** Wide stop → small size. Your excitement gets no vote.
- **Respect the auto de-rating.** High chop shrinks size (Risk Scale < 1.00×). Don't override it.
- **A halt beats a signal.** If the book isn't `Ready`, you don't enter — full stop.
- **Plan the exit before the entry:** T1 = 1R, T2 = 2R, breakeven after T1, chandelier trail on the runner (Playbook §5).

### Weekly review (the engine of this phase)
Block a fixed time each week. Go through every live trade:
- Did you **size by the stop**, correctly, every time? A sizing error is a bigger failure than a losing trade — the loss is variance; the error is a process break.
- Did you **execute the whole plan**, or did you fiddle — move a stop, skip a scale, exit early on a feeling?
- **Thesis verdict vs P&L**, still on separate lines. Look for the pattern: are your "yes" theses the ones that pay? That gap is your read improving (or not).
- Are you **accumulating reps toward the sample**, or cherry-picking comfortable trades? The sample only counts if it's honest.

### Do-not-advance-until gate
- [ ] **Zero sizing errors** across your last ~15 live trades. Not "few" — zero. This is arithmetic; it should be perfect.
- [ ] You executed the **full exit plan** (T1/T2/breakeven/trail) without improvising, on trades that reached those points.
- [ ] You have a **combined replay + live sample approaching 50 journaled reps**, all longs, all Pure Bull.
- [ ] You have run **weekly reviews** consistently — the reviews, not the trades, are what's compounding.

> **The trap of this phase:** boredom. Tiny size makes wins feel pointless and pushes you to size up "to make it interesting" or to add shorts "for opportunities." Both are the ego talking. The job is reps and discipline, not excitement. Size stays tiny until Phase 3 says otherwise — and Phase 3 is about *reading* your sample, not enlarging it.

## Phase 3 — Judge *(Weeks 9–12 and beyond)*

**Goal.** Reach a **50–100 trade long sample** and read it *honestly* — good and bad — using the strategy's own honesty engine. Only from that grounded, longs-first position do you even *consider* whether the short-side validation campaign is worth starting.

**The one skill it builds:** *reading your own sample without flinching* — telling a real, earned edge from a small-sample story you want to be true (Playbook §6, §9; Guide §5, §8).

**Environment:** unchanged. **Tiny live longs, Pure Bull.** You do not scale up size as a reward for reaching Phase 3 — you scale up only if and when your *own* honest numbers, out-of-sample, earn it. Phase 3 is a reading phase before it is anything else.

### The sample-reading routine
Sit with your journal **and** the on-chart Validation panel (Guide §5) together:
- **Count first.** Under ~30 trades the HUD flags **Low Sample**; under ~100 the `!` low-sample flag stands. **Below ~100 trades you know nothing** (Playbook §3). A pretty win rate on 22 trades is an anecdote. Do not make the `!` disappear by lowering the threshold — the only honest way to clear it is *more reps* (Guide §8).
- **Trust the Out-of-Sample row over the headline** (Guide §5). In-sample can flatter; OOS is the honest test. If your own recent, un-rehearsed trades are weak, that's the number that's telling the truth.
- **Read the stress tests.** Ex Best 1 / Ex Best 3 — does your record survive removing your luckiest trade or three? If your edge *is* one trade, you don't have an edge, you have a story.
- **Separate skill from outcome.** Cross-reference thesis verdict against P&L across the whole sample. High-quality reads that happened to lose are the process working; sloppy reads that happened to win are the process failing while variance covers for you.

### The honest verdicts — all three are wins
1. **"My reads are good and my execution is clean."** Then, and only then, consider *gradually* stepping size toward the Playbook's 1%-of-equity standard — earned by evidence, not granted by the calendar.
2. **"My sample is still too thin to say anything."** The correct, common answer before ~100 reps. Stay in Phase 3, keep building. This is not failure; it's the honesty engine doing its job.
3. **"My reads or my discipline are leaking."** The most valuable finding of all. Go back to the phase that fixes it — Phase 1 for reads, Phase 2 for sizing — with no shame. Regression on purpose beats false confidence.

### Only *now*: the short-side question
Reaching Phase 3 with a clean long process **earns you the right to ask** whether to open a short-side validation campaign — it does **not** earn you a short trade. Before a single short:

- [ ] Re-read the **Short Playbook (Playbook §7)** and its warning box in full. The dedicated short system **ran net-negative in the reference backtest.** Running it live is a workflow choice, not evidence.
- [ ] Understand that shorts are studied on the **🔴 PURE BEAR** chart, in isolation, never blended into your longs — and that the **⚪ HYBRID** chart must always be read against Pure Bull, never as your read of the longs (Playbook §8).
- [ ] Commit, *in advance*, to the **A/B/C validation campaign and its pass gates** (Playbook §9): beat longs-only (Arm A) net of costs, beat the mirror (Arm B) on profit factor, **≥ ~100 short trades**, survive the "Ex Best" stress tests, stay stable across walk-forward chunks, and survive the squeeze.
- [ ] Internalize the honest default: **"Don't short" is a valid, honorable strategy until the data clears it.** If you loosen the short filters just to make trades appear, you are gathering sample — *not* proving an edge.

> Shorting is the black-diamond run: losses come faster, squeezes are violent, and the asymmetry is deliberate (Playbook §7). It is the *last* thing you approach, from a position of strength on the tested side — and you may honestly conclude, campaign in hand, that the best short is no short.

### Do-not-consider-shorts-until gate
- [ ] **≥ ~100 journaled long trades.**
- [ ] Your long process is **automatic** — sizing perfect, exit plan followed, stand-downs respected without a second thought.
- [ ] You have read your long sample **honestly**, OOS included, and can state its real edge (or honest absence of one) without spin.
- [ ] Only then do you open a **pre-committed short validation campaign** — with shorts OFF until every gate clears.

## The Ten Reminders (pin these next to the chart)

- [ ] **One strategy, three timeframes, one side, one hundred reps.** Depth before breadth.
- [ ] **Longs first.** The tested workhorse before the unproven experiment — always.
- [ ] **The gate outranks the date.** Being on Week 9 doesn't mean you're ready for Phase 3.
- [ ] **A candidate is not a setup.** A green light is a look, not a trade.
- [ ] **Journal the thesis before the outcome,** or you're just flattering your memory.
- [ ] **Thesis verdict ≠ P&L.** "Was I right" and "did it pay" are different questions.
- [ ] **The stop sets the size. Think in R and % — never dollars.**
- [ ] **A halt beats a signal.** Every time.
- [ ] **Below ~100 trades you know nothing.** The `!` clears with reps, not with a lower threshold.
- [ ] **"Don't short" is honorable** until a pre-committed campaign clears it out-of-sample.
